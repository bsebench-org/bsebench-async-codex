Dear Dr. [last name],

We are writing on behalf of the BSEBench project, an open-source
protocol-layer benchmark for battery state-estimation algorithm
comparison currently under development as part of Oussama Akir's PhD
work at [advisor's institution], under the supervision of Mme Rim.

BSEBench has just completed its M0 milestone : a versioned schema
layer, a protocol ontology grounded in IEC 62660-1 and USABC
standards, an axis taxonomy, two formal task contracts, and a
governance v0.1 document. A key foundation of the methodology is
the PyBaMM synthetic gold standard (roadmap F3) : a known-truth
dataset against which the truth-reconstruction pipeline must recover
SOC within ± 0.5 % over 1000 cycles. PyBaMM is, of course, an
Oxford-led open-source effort.

Beyond PyBaMM, the Oxford Battery Degradation Dataset 1 (under ODC-ODbL
1.0) is on the M3 ingestion list for BSEBench, and the project's
methods seat will need a degradation-modeling perspective during M2
(truth reconstruction) and M5 (RUL task contracts). Oxford is one of
the few institutions in the world that combines a degradation-modeling
research programme with an open data programme and an open simulator.

We would like to invite the Battery Intelligence Lab to nominate a
representative for one of the three institutional seats of the
BSEBench Steering Committee. The governance document is at
`https://github.com/bsebench-org/bsebench-async-codex/blob/demo-k/GOVERNANCE.md`.

The commitment we are asking for:

- A two-year renewable term, ~2 hours per month.
- Participation in the RFC voting process for breaking changes
  (28-day discussion + 7-day vote, asynchronous).
- One open call per quarter (60 minutes, recorded if all members consent).
- Optional co-authorship on the v1.0 release white paper.

What we commit to in return:

- Full attribution of the Oxford Battery Degradation Dataset on every
  leaderboard entry derived from it.
- Explicit reliance on PyBaMM for the F3 gold standard, with PyBaMM
  cited as the analytical-truth source on every grade-A claim.
- Honouring the ODC-ODbL 1.0 share-alike terms on every derived dataset.
- Annual disclosure of project metrics to the Steering Committee.

If you accept, we follow up with a short formal letter and a
one-page onboarding brief. If you decline, the seat remains visibly
empty on the project website until filled.

A response within twenty-one days would let us complete the M0–M1
governance setup. Please direct your reply to
`steering@bsebench.org` (forwarded to Mme Rim and the candidate).

We are very grateful for the Battery Intelligence Lab's role in
making physics-grounded battery modeling open and reproducible.

Respectfully,

Mme Rim, PhD advisor
