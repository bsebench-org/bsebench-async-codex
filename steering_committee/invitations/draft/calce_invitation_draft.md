Dear Dr. [last name],

We are writing on behalf of the BSEBench project, an open-source
protocol-layer benchmark for battery state-estimation algorithm
comparison currently under development as part of Oussama Akir's PhD
work at [advisor's institution], under the supervision of Mme Rim.

BSEBench has just completed its M0 milestone (Foundation Hardening):
a versioned schema layer, a protocol ontology grounded in IEC 62660-1
and USABC standards, an axis taxonomy, two formal task contracts
(`soc_online_estimation_v1`, `soh_capacity_v1`), and a governance v0.1
document. The project will reach v1.0 in approximately seven months
with 30 harmonised datasets, a Closed / Open Division leaderboard, a
PyBaMM synthetic gold standard for self-validation, and a sealed
holdout evaluation layer.

Two of the three foundational datasets in the M0 release are CALCE
datasets : the A123 LFP dynamic profile data and the INR-20R NMC
dynamic profile data. Both are integrated under recipe-only
redistribution — BSEBench hosts the deterministic harmonisation
recipe; the raw files remain at calce.umd.edu. CALCE's open data
program is, alongside NASA-PCoE, one of the two foundations on which
the entire open battery-data ecosystem rests.

We would like to invite CALCE to nominate a representative for one of
the three institutional seats of the BSEBench Steering Committee. The
governance document is at
`https://github.com/bsebench-org/bsebench-async-codex/blob/demo-k/GOVERNANCE.md`.

The commitment we are asking for:

- A two-year renewable term, ~2 hours per month.
- Participation in the RFC voting process for breaking changes
  (28-day discussion + 7-day vote, asynchronous).
- One open call per quarter (60 minutes, recorded if all members consent).
- Optional co-authorship on the v1.0 release white paper.

What we commit to in return:

- Full attribution of CALCE datasets on every leaderboard entry,
  on the project website, and in the v1.0 white paper.
- Explicit citation of the CALCE academic-use language in every
  derived publication that uses CALCE data as a substantial input.
- An annual report to the Steering Committee summarising project metrics.
- No mirroring of CALCE raw files; recipe-only is the default and
  CALCE controls when (and whether) a mirror is ever turned on.

If you accept, we follow up with a short formal letter and a
one-page onboarding brief. If you decline, the seat remains visibly
empty on the project website until filled.

A response within twenty-one days would let us complete the M0–M1
governance setup. Please direct your reply to
`steering@bsebench.org` (forwarded to Mme Rim and the candidate).

We are deeply grateful for CALCE's role in opening this field.

Respectfully,

Mme Rim, PhD advisor
Oussama Akir, PhD candidate
BSEBench project
