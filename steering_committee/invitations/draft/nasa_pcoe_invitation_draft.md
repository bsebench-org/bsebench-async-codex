Dear Dr. [last name],

We are writing on behalf of the BSEBench project, an open-source
protocol-layer benchmark for battery state-estimation algorithm
comparison currently under development as part of Oussama Akir's PhD
work at [advisor's institution], under the supervision of Mme Rim.

BSEBench is in its M0 milestone, having just shipped its authoritative
roadmap, its versioned schema layer (`canonical_dataset_v1.1.0`,
`dataset_card_v1.1.0`, `measurement_provenance_v1.0.0`,
`benchmark_protocol_v1.0.0`), its protocol ontology, and a governance
v0.1 document. The project will reach v1.0 in approximately seven
months, with 30 harmonised datasets, two formal task contracts
(`soc_online_estimation_v1`, `soh_capacity_v1`), and a Closed / Open
Division leaderboard.

The NASA-PCoE Battery Aging dataset (Saha & Goebel, 2007 and
subsequent releases) is one of the three foundational datasets in the
M0 release, integrated under recipe-only redistribution (no mirroring
of the raw files; we host only the deterministic transformation
recipe). Your dataset is the longest-running open battery aging
record in scientific use, and it is one of the three reference
quartets BSEBench will use to validate its truth-reconstruction
pipeline.

We would like to invite the PCoE to nominate a representative for one
of the three institutional seats of the BSEBench Steering Committee.
The full governance document is attached at
`https://github.com/bsebench-org/bsebench-async-codex/blob/demo-k/GOVERNANCE.md`.

The commitment we are asking for:

- A two-year renewable term, ~2 hours per month.
- Participation in the RFC voting process for breaking changes
  (28-day discussion + 7-day vote, asynchronous).
- One open call per quarter (60 minutes, recorded if all members consent).
- Optional co-authorship on the v1.0 release white paper.

What we commit to in return:

- Full attribution of the NASA-PCoE dataset on every leaderboard entry
  derived from it, on the project website, and in the v1.0 white paper.
- Annual disclosure of project metrics (citations, submissions,
  chemistry coverage) to the Steering Committee.
- A 24-month publication lockout on any methodology paper that uses
  PCoE data as a substantial input without a PCoE representative's
  consent (academic courtesy).
- No sponsored placements, no commercial gating of the leaderboard.

If you accept, we will follow up with a short formal letter to nominate
the seat-holder and a one-page onboarding brief. If you decline, the
seat remains visibly empty on the project website until filled — your
decision is recorded only as "declined, [date]" without comment.

A response within twenty-one days would let us complete the M0–M1
governance setup. Please direct your reply to
`steering@bsebench.org` (which is forwarded to Mme Rim and the
candidate).

We are very grateful for the foundational role NASA-PCoE has played
in making this field possible.

Respectfully,

Mme Rim, PhD advisor
Oussama Akir, PhD candidate
BSEBench project
